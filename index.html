What we stand for

Our goal is to strengthen and unite Pravasi individuals and families in Changaramkulam, valuing their cultural legacy while fostering a sense of belonging. We prioritize cultivating togetherness among Pravasis, offering a supportive environment for connections, sharing experiences, and mutual support.

Core Pillars

Our community is built upon five fundamental pillars, representing the principles that guide our efforts in uplifting the Changaramkulam Pravasi Koottayma as a whole:...

  • Financial Stability: We advocate for initiatives and programs that aim to enhance the financial stability of our community at large. By providing financial education, investment opportunities, and entrepreneurship support, we work towards empowering our community economically.
  • Empowerment: Our focus is on empowering the collective through various means such as advocacy, education, skill development, and capacity building. We believe that an empowered community is a catalyst for positive change and sustainable growth.
  • Mutual Support: Central to our ethos is the value of mutual support within our community. We cultivate an environment where members support one another, share knowledge, and collaborate for the common good. Together, we stand stronger.
  • Togetherness: Our community fosters a strong sense of togetherness among our community members. We organize events, communal gatherings, and cultural activities that encourage bonding, understanding, and unity.
  • Social Responsibility: We recognise our duty to society and advocate for social responsibility within our community. Encouraging active participation in social causes, we aim to make a positive impact on the wider society we are an integral part of.

With these core pillars, we envision a strong and unified community that is financially robust, collectively empowered, closely connected, and socially responsible. Through our shared commitment to these principles, we strive for positive transformation and progress, benefiting not only our community but the society at large.
